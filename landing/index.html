The Problem

terraform apply Broke Production. Again.

Every DevOps engineer has a horror story. A config change that looked harmless. A cascade failure at 2am. The plan output looked fine — and then production fell over.

💥

Cascade Failures from "Harmless" Changes

A single config tweak triggers a chain reaction across your infrastructure. By the time you see it, half your services are down.

👀

Single Points of Failure Nobody Noticed

That database with replicas: 1 slipped through code review. You found out at 2am when it went down.

🤷

No Way to Test Resilience Before Deploying

Traditional chaos engineering means breaking production. Regulated industries can't do that. Most teams won't risk it.

The Solution

Simulate Before You Ship

FaultRay builds a mathematical model of your entire system and simulates 2,000+ failure scenarios in memory. Nothing breaks. You just get answers.

1

Graph-Based Cascade Simulation

Model your entire infra as a dependency graph. Inject faults virtually. See exactly what breaks — and what survives.

2

Multi-Layer Availability Limits

Estimate your theoretical maximum uptime across hardware, software, operations, and external dependencies from declared topology. Model-based; accuracy depends on topology fidelity.

3

AI Agent Failure Modeling

First tool to simulate how infrastructure failures cause AI agents to hallucinate, loop, or cascade errors through your system.

How It Works

Three Commands. Full Visibility.

No agents to install. No infrastructure changes. No credentials needed beyond what Terraform already has.

Step 1 — Install

One-line setup

$ pip install faultray
Step 2 — Define

Import or define your infra

$ faultray tf-import
Imported 47 resources from terraform state

$ faultray init
Created infra.yaml template
Step 3 — Simulate

See what breaks

$ faultray simulate
Resilience Score: 72/100
Scenarios tested: 2,000+
Critical: 3 Warning: 12 Passed: 85
